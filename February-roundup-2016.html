February Shelfie:
Stephanie Mastrostefano
 
Stephanie Mastrostefano, Ph.D. student in English, researches how belief systems are marketed to children through everyday media images and social interactions. To learn more about Stephanie's "shelfie" work space, including her study of Disney Pixar's Monsters, Inc., check out our blog.
 
 

Ms. Fembot 2016

The Annenberg School for Communication and Journalism, the Fembot Collective, Ms. Magazine, and UO Center for the Study of Women in Society are sponsoring a two-day event focused on feminist innovation, education and collaboration. Be a part of the Wikipedia Edit-a-Thon writing about marginalized historical figures on day one or attend the Un-conference on Digital Tools for Civic Engagement on day 2. Check out our blog for more.
 

NMCC Co-Sponsored Lecture: Homay King

Homay King (Bryn Mawr) will speak about her new research "Another World is Virtual" on Thursday, February 18th as part of the Cinema Studies lecture series. To learn more visit our blog.
